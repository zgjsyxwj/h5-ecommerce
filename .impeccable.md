# Design Context

## Users

**Primary**: B2B decision makers at Chinese e-commerce companies — VPs of product, CTOs, customer service directors evaluating AI customer service SaaS.

**Context**: Viewing this demo on a desktop browser (likely projected in a meeting, or 1:1 sales call). The page is a *mock storefront* showcasing what their own store could feel like with the AI customer service product enabled. They are skeptical, time-poor, design-literate, and will judge the SaaS's craft by the demo's craft.

**Job to be done**: Within 8 seconds, decide that the SaaS team building this demo has taste and engineering depth. The "smart customer service" entry is the proof point — but the store itself must look *good enough to want to copy*.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **克制 · 精準 · 冷靜** (restrained / precise / calm).

- Voice: editorial, not promotional. Catalog, not catalog-app.
- Tone: confident, quiet, sparing. No exclamation marks, no emoji in product copy, no urgency tactics.
- Emotional goal: viewer thinks *"this team has read every issue of *Apartamento* and shipped a real e-commerce stack"*.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Reference DNA**: Linear / Vercel restraint × Aesop / MUJI catalog × 一条城 / Apartamento editorial.

**Anti-references** (do not look like):
- Taobao / JD / Pinduoduo big-red banner aesthetic
- Chinese SaaS landing pages with cool blue gradients + abstract 3D blobs
- Generic AI-generated shadcn-ish dashboards
- Round chat bubble customer service icons in bottom-right (the #1 AI design tell for e-commerce)

**Theme**: Light. Warm off-white paper background. Daytime B2B desktop context.

**Color palette** (oklch, perceptually uniform):
- `--paper`: oklch(98.5% 0.003 85) — warm off-white background
- `--surface`: oklch(99.5% 0.002 85) — card surface (rare; flat preferred)
- `--ink`: oklch(20% 0.014 240) — primary text, slightly cool
- `--ink-2`: oklch(42% 0.012 240) — secondary text
- `--ink-3`: oklch(60% 0.008 240) — mute caption / SKU
- `--rule`: oklch(88% 0.006 240) — 1px hairline border
- `--rule-soft`: oklch(93% 0.004 240) — softer divider
- `--accent`: oklch(38% 0.085 152) — single deep forest green; the *only* color besides ink. Used for interactive primary (login button, customer service entry, link underlines).

60-30-10: 60% paper, 30% ink in two weights, 10% accent (and even less — accent is rare).

**Typography**:
- Latin / numbers: **Hanken Grotesk** (Google Fonts, weights 300/400/500/700, OFL). Refined neo-grotesque with subtle warmth. Tabular nums on for prices.
- Chinese: system stack `-apple-system, "PingFang SC", "HarmonyOS Sans SC", "Microsoft YaHei"`. Native rendering on Chinese devices is unmatched.
- No monospace anywhere (would read as lazy "techie" shorthand per the brief).

**Spacing**: 4pt scale via semantic tokens. Generous vertical rhythm; tight horizontal for editorial density.

**Motion**: minimal. Stagger fade-in on first paint (70ms each, ease-out-quart, 200ms). Toast slide-up 200ms. Hover transitions 150ms color only. **No bounce, no parallax, no scroll-triggered.**

## Design Principles

1. **Hairline over shadow.** Every separation is a 1px ink-colored rule, never a drop shadow. Shadow is a single hairline tucked against bottom of floating elements only.

2. **Editorial catalog, not card grid.** Products are numbered (No.01—No.08) and laid out as a catalog. Asymmetric, left-aligned, generous whitespace between items.

3. **One accent, used 3 times.** Forest green appears only on: the login button, the customer service entry, and active focus rings. Nowhere else.

4. **No AI fingerprints.** Forbidden: round chat bubble icons, round buttons with emoji, gradient text, glassmorphism, side-stripe borders, generic round shadow cards, oversized icons above headings.

5. **Chinese deserves native fonts.** Don't force a Latin webfont onto Chinese characters. Let the OS render PingFang SC / HarmonyOS Sans SC at their full quality. Tune Latin font's x-height to harmonize with Chinese.
